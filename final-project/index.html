It’s easy to forget what life was like before Amazon Prime. The e-commerce company has taken over the industry as it sells anything from clothing to cleaning supplies to food to a kitchen sick - literally. Once 2005 hit, the company made a drastic change and introduced a service that was first of a kind - Prime.

The Beginning


In 2004, Amazon valued at $18 billion. Fast forward 15 years - Amazon’s worth is $900 billion. Most people credit the company’s success to the creation of Prime, according to an article in Vox.

Back in February of 2005, founder, chairman, president and CEO of the company, Jeff Bezos, released a public letter, announcing the new membership program. For a flat rate of $79, Prime members get “all-you-can-eat” two-day shipping on all orders.

But what if you wanted the order to arrive the next day? No problem. Bezos has a solution - for just $3.99 per item, you can overnight it.

However, some workers at Amazon disagreed with Bezos’ original ideas regarding the membership service. Some thought that the shipping costs would grow far too expensive for the company to keep up with, according to Vox. Workers also raised concerns about actually fulfilling Prime’s promises relating to two-day shipping on all products.

“Very prominent people who are at Amazon today and in high positions told me, ‘You shouldn’t be allowing Jeff to do this,’ and, ‘This is setting a bad example for the company,’” Vijay Ravindran, former Amazon director of ordering, said in an interview with Vox. The original idea of Prime was to offer free shipping, but the order must have a $25 minimum and it takes about 10 days to ship.

Through frequent meetings, Bezos’ team bumped ideas off of each other to perfect Prime’s entire package. Finally, the former Amazon principal engineer, Charlie Ward, came up with the idea to create a flat rate that covered all shipping costs for an entire year.

And so, “all-you-can-eat” shipping was born.

According to Vox, Bezos and his team revolutionized the e-commerce industry. Prime transformed the online shopping experience for members because it offers a variety of products that are easy to purchase. In just a few clicks, members could have almost any product sent to their doorstep in just two days.

But that’s not all. During the last 14 years, Prime perks have increased.

Prime Additions


According to eMarketer, 46% of Prime members purchase something on Amazon at least once per week. Amazon customers are more likely to purchase a product if they are already a Prime subscriber.

In 2018, eMarketer also reported that Amazon has the highest percentage of U.S. e-commerce sales, with 49.1%. EBay trails behind, with only 6.6% of sales.

Some of these sales include electronics, apparel, accessories, furniture, toys, books, music and even movies.

“I don’t remember the last time I walked into a Barnes & Noble,” Amanda Franko, a neuroscience and physiology major at the University of Maryland, said. “I get all of my school supplies and textbooks through my Prime account.”

As Prime’s popularity grew since 2005, Bezos added many benefits to the membership. They include Prime Video, Prime Pantry, Prime Music, Prime Photos, Prime Now, Prime Reading and Prime Wardrobe.


Prime Video
Expands its access to thousands of television series and movies.

Prime Pantry
Members can receive one box full of non-perishable grocery store items for a flat fee.

Prime Music
Subsribers have access to thousands of songs via smartphone or tablet.

Prime Photos
With subscription, members get access to store photos and videos.

Prime Now
At this point, members can get free two-hour delivery and one-hour delivery for a low price.

Prime Reading
Amazon introduces a virtual platform for reading and browsing through thousands of different books.

Amazon Visa Card
Prime members can apply for a visa card to use as a form of payment.

Prime Wardrobe
Prime members can purchase clothes through Amazon, try them on and return them without any additional charges.

Amazon Key
To ensure higher security, members can purchase a video camera that monitors Amazon delivery.

In particular, Prime Video is a crucial selling point for Amazon’s international customers. Prime members primarily in Japan, Germany and the United Kingdom have increased, according to data released by Morgan Stanley.

To account for all of these added benefits, the company decided to increase the membership fee by 25.32% back in March 2014. Despite the increase to $99 annually, this didn’t stop Prime subscribers from renewing another year. In fact, the Consumer Intelligence Research Partners (CIRP) saw a high retention rate, regardless of the price.

“CIRP estimates that currently 73% of trial subscribers pay for the first full year of Amazon Prime membership. 91% of first-year paid subscribers renew for a second year, and 96% of second-year paid subscribers renew for a third year,” according to an article published by the HuffPost.

After the increase in membership price, Prime started offering same-day delivery. On eligible orders, customers could receive their purchase later that same day.

“I depend on my Amazon Prime account for my everyday needs. I’m a music teacher, so it’s very easy for me to use Amazon as a way to get resources for the classroom in a timely and efficient manner, for an inexpensive price,” Alex Sanford, a music teacher based out of New Hampshire, said in a phone interview.

Prime also introduced the Amazon Key in October 2017, which allows customers to purchase a “Video Doorbell and Camera.”

“See who’s there. Then let them in.”

The doorbell allows customers to see the delivery worker drop and then can let them into their home to leave the package inside rather than outside.

And just about a year ago, Prime’s annual membership fee increased to $119.

One month prior to the 20.2% increase in price, Prime began offering trunk delivery. So now, you can not only get a package delivered to inside of your house, but also your car’s trunk is a new delivery spot.

CIRP and Statistica released that in June 2017, there were 85 million U.S. Prime subscribers. That’s a 107.32% increase from March 2015.

The Future


In 2017, Amazon acquired Whole Foods Market Inc. (WFM), and through Prime Now, subscribers can grocery shop online at WFM with the option of one or two hour delivery.

But, customers still prefer to shop at different grocery stores because of the perception of higher prices at WFM, according to a study conducted by Morgan Stanley.

To break this perception and barrier to shopping at WFM, Prime is creating ways to attract more customers to shop at there through their attractive benefits offered in Prime Now. Some benefits include food sales, free two-day delivery and free same-day delivery in certain regions.

How many US households are expected to have Prime?

Just when you think Amazon had enough perks with Prime, a future benefit associated with the service was released - Prime Air. The future drone delivery system that doesn’t have a release date yet will deliver products under five pounds. In just 30 minutes, customers will receive their product from the sky.




“The vehicle is completely autonomous. It lands by itself. It navigates by itself. It can fly more than 50 mph,” Bezos said at the 2016 Pathfinder Awards. “It’s really an extraordinary technology.”
